MY FAVOURITE MEAL

There are a lot of foods in the world but my favorite meal of all time is YAM PORRIDGE/YAM POTTAGE (ASARO) because it tastes so yummy and great.

Yam pottage/porridge with is locally known as Asaro is a very delicious Yam recipe though it’s eaten by most tribes in Nigeria however, it is more common in the Yoruba-speaking parts of Nigeria. Yam pottage/porridge is soft and it tastes so good with the combination of ingredients. It’s also very easy to make. If you love YAM, it’s a must-try recipe!

Asaro is made from Puna Yam which is boiled till it’s tender and it’s cooked in a blend of Peppers, Tomatoes, onions, palm oil, and seasoning. While the dish is more common in the Yoruba-speaking region of Nigeria, it is still a meal enjoyed by the majority alike each with their own regional variation.

It is nicknamed “Asaro elepo rede rede” meaning yam porridge boldly colored with fresh palm oil and garnished to perfection.

Yam Porridge/ Yam Pottage Recipe

The list of Ingredients needed are:

S/N Ingredients Measurements/Quantity
1. Yam 1 Tuber
2. Red Bell Pepper 1 Bell
3. Tomato 1(large)
4. Habenero Pepper 1
5. Onion 1(medium)
6. Water 3 Cups
7. Fresh Palm Oil 1/2 cup
8. CrayFish 2 tsp
9. Salt 1 tsp
10. Chicken Bouillion Powder 2 tsp
11. Mackerel Fish Salmon/
StockFish or
driedfish 		15 Ounces
12. Basil or Parsley or
any leafy vegetable
(spinach,kale or ugwu) 		Chopped

Instructions/Steps/Methods:

  1. Peel and cut the Yam into medium-sized chunks. Rinse a couple of times until the water is no longer cloudy. Set aside
  2. Blend the bell pepper, habanero, tomato, and half onion.
  3. Put the yams inside a pot, and add the blended sauce, salt, bouillon powder, and water. Cover and let it cook for 10 minutes on medium to high heat
  4. Stir in the palm oil, sliced onions, and crayfish, cover, and leave to cook for an additional 10 minutes.
  5. Mash the yams with a potato masher or a wooden spoon. I love to mash some yams and keep some chunks. It gives more texture!
  6. Add the fish (You can use whatever addition you prefer, like smoked fish or crab meat).
  7. Allow it to simmer for an additional 3 to 5 minutes.
  8. Stir in the herbs like basil, or leafy greens like spinach, kale, or ugwu) and enjoy while still hot!

NB: You can serve with the following

  • Stew "Ata Dindin"
  • Seafood
  • Turkey
  • Chicken e.t.c
